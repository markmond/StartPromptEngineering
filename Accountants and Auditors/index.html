    Prompt Engineering Playbook for Accountants and Auditors

    This playbook provides a structured approach to using prompt engineering techniques, tailored to the tasks and activities of accountants and auditors. It focuses on practical examples, strategies, and ethical considerations for interacting with large language models (LLMs).

    1. Core Concepts

    • Financial Systems as Data Sources: Think of financial records, audit reports, and accounting systems as structured data you interact with.
    • Data Types: Transactions, accounts, compliance records, and performance metrics.
    • Relationships: Financial data often connects, such as payroll linked to tax filings or expenses grouped by department.

    • Unique Identifiers: Use details like transaction IDs or account numbers for specificity.

    • Structured Thinking: Formulate prompts that logically reflect how accountants analyze data—breaking down tasks into clear steps or focused queries.

    2. Privacy and Ethics in Prompting

    Caveats for Privacy and Sensitive Information

    • Confidentiality: Never input sensitive or proprietary financial data, personal information, or client details into an LLM without ensuring the platform adheres to strict privacy and security standards.
    • Data Masking: Replace sensitive information with placeholders or anonymized data.
    • Example: Replace "John Doe" with "Client A" or use sample values instead of real financial figures.
    • Regulatory Compliance: Ensure that prompts and outputs comply with laws like GDPR, HIPAA, or financial industry regulations.

    Ethical Use of LLMs

    • Transparency: Inform stakeholders if LLMs are being used to assist with reports or analyses.
    • Validation: Verify all outputs independently to avoid reliance on potentially inaccurate or biased information.
    • Bias Awareness: Recognize that LLMs may inadvertently introduce biases or hallucinations (fabricated information) into outputs.
    • Organizational Restrictions: Respect organizational policies that limit or prohibit the use of generative AI for specific functions, especially in regulated industries.

    3. Prompting Techniques

    Basic Prompts

    • Use direct questions or requests to retrieve insights.
    • Example: "What are the main risks identified in this audit report?"
    • Example: "Summarize the findings related to asset utilization for the last quarter."

    Detailed Prompts

    • Include clear instructions to guide responses.
    • Example: "List all compliance issues identified in audit findings, along with their financial impact and recommended actions."

    Focused Prompts

    • Narrow the scope of your prompt to specific criteria.
    • Example: "Highlight all discrepancies in financial records exceeding $5,000 and suggest possible causes."

    Structured Prompts

    • Organize your prompts to reflect a specific output format.
    • Example: "Provide a summary of this financial report with these headings: Overview, Key Findings, Risks, and Recommendations."

    4. Advanced Prompting Techniques

    Role-based Prompts

    • Assign roles to guide how the model responds.
    • Example: "You are a financial auditor preparing a report. Identify the key financial risks observed during your review."

    Instructional Prompts

    • Provide step-by-step instructions.
    • Example: "Explain how to prepare adjusting journal entries to correct discrepancies in a trial balance."

    Few-shot Prompts

    • Include examples to demonstrate the desired output.
    • Example:
      • Input: "Here are examples of audit findings:
      • Duplicate invoices in vendor payments.
      • Non-compliance with financial reporting standards.
        Based on these examples, generate similar findings for this data."

    Dynamic Prompts

    • Use context-specific or real-time data in your prompts.
    • Example: "Using this data—Revenue: $5,000,000; Expenses: $4,200,000; Taxes Paid: $400,000—generate a summary of financial performance for the quarter."

    5. Optimization Techniques

    Iterative Refinement

    • Start with a broad prompt and refine it for better results.
    • Example:
      • Initial: "Analyze inefficiencies in the payroll system."
      • Refined: "Analyze the payroll system for inefficiencies, such as overpayments, underpayments, or delays, and provide actionable recommendations."

    Error Analysis

    • Identify gaps or errors in responses and adjust prompts to improve clarity.
    • Example:
      • Problem: The output includes irrelevant details.
      • Solution: Specify, "Only include discrepancies above $10,000 and focus on compliance-related issues."

    Prompt Engineering

    • Break down complex tasks into manageable steps.
    • Example: "Create a checklist for evaluating financial and operational controls in an accounting system, focusing on fraud prevention, policy adherence, and efficiency."

    6. Applications for Accountants and Auditors

    Financial Summaries

    • "Summarize the key points of this audit report, focusing on critical financial risks and operational inefficiencies."

    Compliance and Risk Analysis

    • "Identify compliance gaps and financial risks from this set of financial statements."

    Budget Insights

    • "Compare actual expenses to budgeted costs and highlight any variances, along with possible reasons."

    Data Verification

    • "Cross-check payroll records against tax filings to identify mismatches in reported earnings and tax payments."

    Tax Preparation

    • "Calculate the taxes owed for a business using the following information: Revenue, deductions, and tax rates."

    Fraud Detection

    • "Analyze this set of financial transactions to identify potential indicators of fraud or non-compliance."

    Audit Reporting

    • "Prepare a detailed audit report summarizing findings, including inefficiencies in cash flow, compliance issues, and recommended actions."

    7. Step-by-Step Framework for Prompting

    Step 1: Define Your Goal

    • Clearly articulate what you need to know or achieve.
    • Example: "I need a summary of discrepancies in financial statements for Q3."

    Step 2: Identify Key Details

    • Provide context and focus areas in your prompt.
    • Example: "Focus on discrepancies above $10,000 and include recommendations."

    Step 3: Choose a Structure

    • Specify how the output should be organized.
    • Example: "Summarize findings using these sections: Overview, Key Issues, and Recommendations."

    Step 4: Review and Refine

    • Analyze the output and tweak the prompt for precision.
    • Example: "Add more detail about the financial impact of these discrepancies."

    Best Practices for Accountants and Auditors

    1. Avoid Sensitive Data: Always use anonymized or sample data in prompts.
    2. Validate Outputs: Double-check generated results for accuracy and reliability.
    3. Stay Compliant: Ensure usage aligns with industry regulations and organizational policies.
    4. Educate Stakeholders: Communicate the role of LLMs in your work and highlight their limitations.
    5. Use Responsibly: Consider ethical implications and transparency when applying AI tools.

    This is just a primer to get you started. The key is to start and iterate on the outputs you get.
